Sloppy Joe Sandwich

It is of my utmost (and unimportant) opinion that not much comes close to beating a well-made sloppy joe Sandwich for a pleasant and filling lunch. With all of the robust notes from the sauce smothering the hearty beef filling, a nice sloppy joe covers almost all of the bases. Without further ado, let's get this rolling!

Ingredients:

As usual, we will start with an ingredients list as follows:

  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1/2 small green bell pepper, seeded and finely diced
  • 1 tbsp worchestershire sauce
  • 1 tbsp yellow mustard
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • 15 oz can tomato sauce
  • 1 lb lean ground beef, 85%-90% lean
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp ground black pepper
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 4 hamburger buns, toasted if desired

Now that we've got that all covered, let's put it all together!

Directions

  1. Finely dice the onion and seed and finely dice the green pepper
  2. In a bowl, combine the Worchestershire sauce, mustard, water, brown sugar and tomato sauce.
  3. Using a large skillet over medium/high heat, add olive oil and ground beef. Saute the beef for about 5 minutes until cooked through and no longer pink, breaking it up with a spatula.
  4. ASeason beef with salt and pepper and add in the diced peppers and onion. Cook another 5 minutes until the veggies are tender and the beef is browned.
  5. Add the minced garlic and saute 30 seconds until fragrant, stirring constantly. Add in the sauce and bring to a light boil. Reduce heat to low and simmer, uncovered, for about 10 minutes.
  6. Season to taste with salt and pepper.
  7. Use plain OR toasted buns and enjoy!
